Don’t go to bed and close your sleepy eyes until you have assessed your daytime deeds. Where did I go wrong? What did I do? What’s been left undone? From first to last review the day appreciating what you did well yet urge yourself to do better tomorrow on those things that went poorly.
— Epictetus: Key Discourses

When I deployed with the Army in 2020, my father, Pat Helmers, gave me a journal. Inside was a page with instructions on how to journal like a stoic:

Review the day 3 times, beginning to end:

  1. What did you do well today?
  2. What did you do poorly?
  3. What should I do to become a better version of myself?

I took this and ran with it. Here's an app to help journalling from your keyboard.
